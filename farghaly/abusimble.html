Abu Simbel Temple

The Abu Simbel Temple in Aswan Governorate is considered one of the most famous archaeological temples in Egypt. The importance of the Great Temple of Abu Simbel is due to its association with the phenomenon of the sun perpendicular to the face of the statue of Pharaoh Ramesses II twice a year, the first corresponding to October 22 and the second to February 22 of each year. This temple was built by King Ramses II Ramesses II (about 1303 BC - 1213 BC), the most famous king of Egypt from the era of the New Kingdom. It is believed that he sat on the throne when he was in his late teens and ruled Egypt from 1279 BC to 1213 BC for a period of 67 years.
